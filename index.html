Chapter 02

Legal Registration

Recommended structure: Sole Proprietorship. Use your own PAN — lowest compliance, easiest to start.

1
Free / ₹107

PAN Card + Aadhaar

Foundation documents for all registrations. Apply PAN on tin.tin.nsdl.com (₹107). Ensure Aadhaar is updated. Download both as PDF — mandatory for every next step.

📍 NSDL / UTI Portal⏱ 5–7 days
2
Free — Instant

Udyam / MSME Registration

10-minute self-declaration. Just PAN + Aadhaar. Unlocks government schemes, APEDA/Spices Board subsidies, priority bank loans, and trade fair support.

📍 udyamregistration.gov.in⏱ Instant
3
₹500 — MOST CRITICAL

IEC — Import Export Code

10-digit code mandatory for every export transaction. No IEC = No Export. Apply fully online on DGFT portal. Arrives by email in 1–3 working days. Documents needed: PAN, Aadhaar, bank cancelled cheque, photo and digital signature.

📍 dgft.gov.in⏱ 1–3 days
4
Free

GST Registration

Exports are zero-rated under GST — you get full Input Tax Credit refund on raw materials. Register even below ₹20L threshold to claim export refund benefits. Apply online at gst.gov.in.

📍 gst.gov.in⏱ 3–7 days
5
Min ₹5,000

Current Bank Account + AD Code

Open at HDFC or SBI (best FEMA/forex support). Ask for "Current Account with export facility." After opening, get AD Code letter from bank and register at nearest Customs ICD/Port — critical for receiving foreign payments.

📍 Bank Branch + Customs ICD⏱ 1–2 days
6
₹500 – ₹5,000

Product-Specific Registrations

FSSAI for all food products (₹2,000–5,000). APEDA for agri/processed food (₹5,000). Spices Board for turmeric, pepper, cardamom (₹500). Apply only after IEC + GST are completed.

📍 foscos.fssai.gov.in / apeda.gov.in⏱ 15–60 days
⚠️
AD Code Alert: After opening bank account, get the AD Code letter and register at your nearest Customs ICD/Port. Without this, foreign payments will NOT credit to your account even if received. Takes 1–2 working days. Completely free.

Chapter 04

Export Documentation

Every document you generate per shipment — what it is, who uses it, and why it matters.

01
Pre-Shipment

Proforma Invoice

Your quotation before order confirmation. Product specs, quantity, price in USD/EUR, payment terms, Incoterms (FOB/CIF), validity period. Create free in Zoho Invoice or Google Docs.

02
Pre-Shipment

Commercial Invoice

Final billing document after order confirmed. Must include IEC, GSTIN, HS Code, country of origin. Used by destination customs to calculate import duties.

03
Pre-Shipment

Packing List

Carton-by-carton contents breakdown. Includes carton numbers, net/gross weight, dimensions, contents per carton. Customs physically verifies against this list.

04
At Shipment

Shipping Bill

India's mandatory customs export declaration. Filed on ICEGATE portal by your CHA. Contains IEC, GSTIN, AD Code, HS code, FOB value, duty drawback / RoDTEP claim.

05
At Shipment

Bill of Lading / Airway Bill

Issued by shipping line or airline. Title document — whoever holds original B/L owns the goods. Critical for LC payments — submitted to bank to release funds.

06
Post-Shipment

Certificate of Origin

Proves goods are Made in India. Issued by FIEO or Chamber of Commerce. Required for preferential duty under India FTAs. Fee: ₹200–500 per certificate.

07
Large Orders

Letter of Credit (LC)

Bank-backed payment guarantee for orders above ₹5L. Buyer opens LC → bank notifies you → ship → present docs → bank pays. Beginners: use 50% advance first.

💡
Pro Tip: Hire a CHA (Customs House Agent) for your first 5 shipments. They handle Shipping Bill, ICEGATE filing and customs clearance for ₹2,000–5,000 per shipment. Find at chaagentindia.com.
Chapter 05

Banking & Payments

How to receive money safely from international buyers and protect against fraud.

Safest

100% Advance (T/T)

Wire transfer before shipment. Best for new buyers and small orders. SWIFT fee: ₹500–2,000.

Best for: First-time buyers
Very Safe

50% + 50% Terms

50% advance, 50% before shipment. Ideal for repeat buyers. Protects you while building trust.

Best for: Regular buyers
Safe

Letter of Credit

Bank-backed guarantee. Fee: 0.5–1% of order value. Document precision required — one error = delay.

Best for: Orders above ₹5L
Medium

Documents Against Payment

Buyer pays to collect shipping documents. Bank manages collection. Fee: ~0.5%.

Best for: Established buyers
Low Risk

PayPal / Wise

For samples and orders under $500. Fee: 3–5%. Fast and easy but not suitable for bulk trade.

Best for: Samples & micro-orders
⛔ Avoid

Open Account / Credit

Ship first, get paid later. Never for beginners. Extremely high fraud risk with unknown buyers.

Avoid: Until 2+ years in business

Fraud Prevention — 8 Rules

✓ Verify buyer on LinkedIn — company age, posts, employees
✓ Google "[company name] scam" before engaging
✓ Never ship without minimum 30% advance payment
✓ Use Alibaba Trade Assurance for first orders
✓ Request buyer's business registration certificate
✓ Never share banking details via WhatsApp without call verification
✓ Check on D&B (Dun & Bradstreet) or Creditsafe
✓ Keep all FIRCs from bank — needed for GST refund claims

Chapter 07

Closing Deals

Pricing formulas, negotiation rules, sample process and the complete deal closure sequence.

07A — PRICING

Pricing Formula

EX-WORKS
Cost of goods + Packaging + 20–30% Profit
↓ + Inland transport + CHA charges + Port charges
FOB Price ★ Quote this to most buyers
All costs up to loading on vessel at Indian port
↓ + Marine insurance + Ocean / Air freight
CIF Price
All-inclusive at destination port

Always quote FOB or CIF. Never EX-Works to large buyers — you lose control of the shipment quality and routing.

07B — SAMPLES

Sample Sending Process

1
Ask buyer to pay DHL/FedEx courier charges. If they refuse — it's a red flag, walk away.
2
Send max 100–250g via courier. Label: "Commercial Sample — No Commercial Value"
3
Follow up 5 days after delivery with call or email asking for quality feedback.
4
Positive response → send Proforma Invoice → negotiate terms → get advance → ship.
07C — NEGOTIATION

Negotiation Rules

✓ Do This
  • Quote 15–20% above your minimum
  • Offer volume discounts in writing
  • Negotiate MOQ, not just price
  • Always reply within 24 hours
  • Send detailed spec sheet PDF
✗ Don't Do This
  • Undercut your price drastically
  • Accept 100% post-delivery payment
  • Make verbal price commitments
  • Ignore Incoterms in quotes
  • Skip written contracts
07D — CLOSING

Deal Closing Sequence

  1. Send sample → wait for quality feedback from buyer
  2. Send Proforma Invoice with 15-day validity
  3. Follow up after 5 days if no response received
  4. Negotiate MOQ, unit price, and payment terms
  5. Receive Purchase Order (PO) on buyer's letterhead
  6. Issue final confirmed PI → collect 50% advance
  7. Begin production or sourcing from supplier
  8. Ship → send full document set → collect balance

08A — FORWARDER

Choosing a Freight Forwarder

  1. Get quotes from 3+ forwarders — compare total landed cost, not just freight
  2. Ask if they handle CHA, documentation, ICEGATE filing
  3. Check IATA (air) / IBA (sea) membership for credibility
  4. Compare: FedEx Trade, DHL Global Forwarding, DB Schenker
  5. Use Freight Tiger or Freightos for sea rate comparison
  6. Build long-term relationship with 1 reliable forwarder
08B — PACKAGING

Export Packaging Requirements

  • Outer carton: Double-wall corrugated, 3-ply minimum. Mark product name, net/gross weight, origin on all sides.
  • Inner: Vacuum-sealed / food-grade for all food products
  • Labels: Must comply with destination laws — FDA labels for USA, CE marking for EU
  • Pallet: Heat-treated ISPM-15 compliant wood with fumigation certificate
  • HS Code: Mark on outer carton label for customs fast-track clearance

Chapter 11

Risk Management

The 10 mistakes that kill new exporters — and exactly how to avoid each one.

RISK 01

Shipping Without Advance

Never ship without at least 30% advance. Even with "trusted" buyers you met online.

RISK 02

Wrong HS Code

Incorrect code = wrong duty = shipment held at customs. Verify at icegate.gov.in every time.

RISK 03

Skipping AD Code

Foreign payment won't credit without AD Code at customs. Register before first shipment.

RISK 04

No Export Insurance

ECGC Nirvik scheme covers payment default. ₹500–2,000 per shipment. Worth every rupee.

RISK 05

Missing GST Refunds

File GSTR-1 and claim input tax refund on all exports. Real money most beginners miss.

RISK 06

EX-Works to Large Buyers

They'll use cheap forwarders, damage goods, then blame you. Always quote FOB or CIF.

RISK 07

LC Discrepancies

Any document error = payment blocked. Get CA or banker to review every LC carefully.

RISK 08

Overpromising Delivery

Add 7–10 day buffer always. Late delivery is the #1 reason buyers don't reorder.

RISK 09

Missing Duty Drawback

Claim RoDTEP / Duty Drawback on every shipment. Your CHA can help file these claims.

RISK 10

Mixed Finances

Always use a separate current account for business. Mixing creates GST audit nightmares.

🚨 Buyer Red Flags — Walk Away If You See These

Offers unusually high prices without negotiating at all
Asks for more free samples before placing any actual order
Wants payment routed via an "agent" or third party
Poorly formatted PO with no company letterhead or logo
Requests your personal bank account instead of business
Pressures you to ship before documentation is complete
